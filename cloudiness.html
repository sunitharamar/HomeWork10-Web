Cloudiness

There are three broad bands where Earth's skies are most likely to be cloudy: a narrow strip near the equator and two wider strips in the mid-latitudes. The band near the equator is a function of the large scale circulation patterns—or Hadley cells—present in the tropics.

Cloudiness is spread from 0 to 100% level across the entire range of Latitude.
